Enrollments by Gender and Races

In 2020 there were 49,139 students enrolled in Orlando, FL, 39% men and 61% women. By race, the largest number of students enrolled was concentrated in Hispanic or Latino with 18,839 records, of which 62% were women and 38% men.

Race and Ethnicity

In 2020, there were 1.54 times more White (Non-Hispanic) residents (103k people) in Orlando, FL than any other race or ethnicity. There were 66.4k Black or African American (Non-Hispanic) and 61k White (Hispanic) residents, the second and third most common ethnic groups. 32.7% of the people in Orlando, FL are hispanic (93k people). The following chart shows the 7 races represented in Orlando, FL as a share of the total population.

Occupations

From 2019 to 2020, employment in Orlando, FL grew at a rate of 1.61%, from 155k employees to 157k employees. The most common job groups, by number of people living in Orlando, FL, are Sales & Related Occupations (20,757 people), Office & Administrative Support Occupations (17,588 people), and Management Occupations (17,271 people). This chart illustrates the share breakdown of the primary jobs held by residents of Orlando, FL.

Median Earnings by Industry

The industries with the best median earnings for men in 2020 are Information ($59,881), Professional, Scientific, & Management, & Administrative & Waste Management Services ($52,576), and Manufacturing ($52,212). The industries with the best median earnings for women in 2020 are Public Administration ($47,129), Information ($42,008), and Finance & Insurance, & Real Estate & Rental & Leasing ($40,888).
